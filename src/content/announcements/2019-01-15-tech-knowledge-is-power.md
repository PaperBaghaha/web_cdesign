<p><a href="https://Engineering.Purdue.edu/cdesign/wp/tech-knowledge-is-power-by-mung-chiang-dean-of-the-college-of-engineering-at-purdue-university/capture-25/" rel="attachment wp-att-5474"><img loading="lazy" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-5474" src="/assets/img/news/content/Capture-1024x677.jpg" alt="Capture" width="550" height="364" /></a></p>
<p><em>&#8220;As our world is re-engineered, more people need to learn how engineers analyze, design and operate. Engineering concepts such as modular design and feedback control can become part of citizens&#8217; vernacular, just like inflation and trade deficit from economics. Sure, it&#8217;s interesting. But it&#8217;s also integral to daily life.</em></p>
<p><em>As the dean of one of America&#8217;s preeminent engineering schools, I&#8217;m not advocating a complete makeover of college curricula. But I do believe a base understanding of certain big ideas would begin to give people the oars to navigate technology&#8217;s rapids. A glimpse of how encryption for privacy works, or an effort to demystify the cloud, or a whiff of context around machine-learning algorithms could make technology more accessible and something to embrace rather than fear.&#8221; </em></p>
<p>&#8211; by Mung Chiang</p>
