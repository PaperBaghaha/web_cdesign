<p>The Purdue Table Tennis Club recently placed first in the NCTTA Fall Divisional Tournament, strengthening the team&#8217;s chances of competing in the regional and national competitions.</p>
<p>&#8220;Anyone in the community can play,&#8221; Karthik Ramani, the Donald W. Feddersen Distinguished professor of mechanical engineering and faculty advisor for the Purdue Table Tennis Club said. &#8220;It&#8217;s five dollars (to play), so they can just come and play with the club. They advantage is they get some coaching with experts&#8230;or they just have fun&#8230;But it&#8217;s open to anybody in the community.&#8221;</p>
<p>Being the self-proclaimed &#8220;underdogs&#8221; of the NCTTA Fall Divisional Tournament, the win was not expected, especially when facing off against some of the historically top-placing teams in the Midwest. That being said, the win was a welcome and much celebrated one.</p>
